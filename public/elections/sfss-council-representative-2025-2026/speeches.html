CSSS 2025-2026 SFSS Council Representative Election

What is an SFSS Council Representative? You can read more about it in the CSSS's Constitution.

Voting for this election will be conducted through SFU's simplyvoting platform. If you are elligible, you will be sent an email with instructions on how to vote (login to sfu.simplyvoting.com and select this election).

You are elligible to vote if you are a declared majors, minors, or honours in any program of study offered at the undergraduate level by the School of Computing Science, or are currently enrolled in any undergraduate course offered by the School of Computing Science.

The following speeches are provided by the candidates.

Candidate Name: Chitransh Motwani
Candidate Speech
Dear CSSS Members,
My name is Chitransh Motwani, and I am excited to run for your 2025-2026 CSSS Council Representative. As someone who has been deeply involved in student governance and event organization at SFU, I understand the importance of securing funding, advocating for student needs, and ensuring that CSSS remains an active and thriving community.
Why I’m the Best Candidate for This Role
I currently serve as the Vice President of Events & Student Affairs at the Simon Fraser Student Society (SFSS), where I represent over 26,000 students and oversee event planning, budgeting, and community engagement. In this role, I have successfully:
  • Secured funding for large-scale events, negotiating with SFSS Council and other stakeholders to bring student initiatives to life.
  • Led high-impact events, including club nights, cultural festivals, and networking sessions, ensuring students get value from their society membership.
  • Advocated for student resources, working directly with the SFSS Council to push for more funding and better student services.
Beyond SFSS, I was also Co-President of the Indian Student Federation, where I managed sponsorships, fundraising, and large-scale community events. My experience navigating financial approvals, grant applications, and budget discussions equips me with the skills to effectively advocate for increased SFSS funding for CSSS events, workshops, and student initiatives.
What I Will Do as Your CSSS Council Representative
  • Secure More Funding for CSSS Events & Initiatives – I know exactly how SFSS Council evaluates funding requests, and I will use this knowledge to ensure CSSS gets the grants it needs.
  • Strengthen CSSS Representation at SFSS Council – I will be an active voice for computing science students, ensuring our needs are prioritized in SFSS decisions.
  • Improve Transparency & Communication – I will provide regular updates on Council decisions, seek your input before key votes, and ensure that every CSSS member stays informed.
My experience, my track record, and my passion for student advocacy make me the ideal candidate for this role. Let’s work together to secure more funding, create more opportunities, and make CSSS stronger than ever.
I would be honored to have your support!
Chitransh Motwani
Candidate Name: Matthew Wong
Candidate Speech

Hello everyone,

My name is Matthew Wong, and I’m currently the President of SFU Surge, SFU’s leading tech club focused on empowering students through innovation, learning, and community. Over the past three years, I’ve had the opportunity to lead and organize some of the university’s most impactful student-run events, including JourneyHacks, our beginner-friendly hackathon; StormHacks, a 500 person annual hackathon that brings together students across Canada; and the Surge Projects Program, where we help students go from idea to deployment in cross-functional teams.

These experiences weren’t just about running events, they were about building trust with students, collaborating across faculties, and navigating the realities of what it means to represent people. I’ve worked closely with SFSS Council before to secure funding, propose changes to event policy, and advocate for better support for student-led initiatives. I know how important it is for Council to be a space where student voices aren’t just heard, but turned into action. If elected as your CSSS Council Representative, I’ll bring the same level of commitment, transparency, and initiative I’ve brought to Surge. I’ll represent our community with integrity, push for more opportunities for student growth, and ensure that our concerns as computing science students are reflected in decisions at the highest level.

Thank you for your time, and I’d be honoured to earn your vote.

Candidate Name: Surya Murali
Candidate Speech

Candidate Speech for CSSS Council Representative

Hey everyone! My name is Surya Murali, and I’m running to be your next CSSS Council Representative.

Over the past year, I’ve been lucky to be a part of the CSSS Community — especially during CSSS Frosh 2024, where I helped across almost every event. Frosh was a huge success, and that’s thanks to the incredible work of our execs. I saw firsthand how much they put on the line — their time, effort, and passion — all for the sake of building something amazing for our community.

But I also saw the frustrations behind the scenes — slow processes, budget disputes, and back-and-forths with SFSS. I want to step in and make sure that kind of stress doesn’t get in the way of the things that make CSSS great.

First, I’ll be a strong voice during SFSS Council meetings— because that’s where the real decisions happen. It’s not about where funds are “allocated” on paper — it’s about how councillors vote when CSSS asks for support. I’ve done my homework, gone through SFSS financials, and I know how to advocate clearly and effectively. If elected, I’ll be that person who gets others on board — so CSSS events get the votes they need.

Second, I’ll work to build stronger connections with other DSUs across SFU. CSSS is one of the most active student societies on campus — we should be leading the charge in bringing students together. I want us to collaborate more with other departments, host cross-faculty events, and make CSSS the hub for innovation and community at SFU.

Finally, I’m running on transparency and presence. That means keeping you updated on what’s happening in Council, being available for feedback, and spending more time in the common room to connect with everyone — not just during campaign season, but throughout the year. I know that sometimes it can feel hard to speak up or be heard, and I want to make sure no one feels left out of the loop.

I’ve been on the ground, I’ve done the research, and I care deeply about making sure CSSS continues to grow without being held back by bureaucracy. If you want a rep who’s experienced, approachable, and ready to advocate — I’d be honoured to have your vote.

Let’s make it happen — together. Thank you!

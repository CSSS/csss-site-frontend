Voting for this election will be conducted through SFU's simplyvoting platform. If you are elligible, you will be sent an email with instructions on how to vote (login to sfu.simplyvoting.com and select this election).

You are elligible to vote if you are a declared majors, minors, or honours in any program of study offered at the undergraduate level by the School of Computing Science, or are currently enrolled in any undergraduate course offered by the School of Computing Science.

[0] Treasurer (2 candidates)

Candidate Name: Matthew Liu
Candidate Speech

Hello all!

My name is Matthew, and I’m a 3rd year Computing Science student running for Treasurer of the CSSS for 2025-2025!

I’ve been an active member of the CS community, contributing to student clubs like SFU Robot Soccer and SFU SubVision, where I’ve worked on designing and building software in team-based engineering environments. I’ve also regularly attended CSSS events like Board Games Nights, Hackathons, and FROSH, so I understand firsthand how important it is for funding to flow back into our student community in fun, engaging, and meaningful ways.

Outside of academics, I stay involved in my local community by helping to clean and decorate our space for events and gatherings. I’ve found this to be a great way to give back and support the people around me. It’s taught me the value of pitching in behind the scenes to help make things run smoothly - something I’d bring to the Treasurer role as well.

As Treasurer, I want to make sure our student fees are put to good use by supporting events, initiatives, and resources that create opportunities for everyone in our community. Having attended CSSS General Meetings, I have learned about the importance of communication between execs, performing cash motions for events, and the administrative work that goes on to keep the CSSS running. Furthermore, I believe in financial transparency and smart budgeting. I’ll work with the exec team to make sure every dollar we spend makes a meaningful impact for the CS community here.

Here are some of my goals for the upcoming term:

  • Maintain a transparent and up-to-date budget for the CSSS community.
  • Work with the exec team to ensure bi-weekly board game nights, hackathons, Frosh, and other events are well-funded and successful.
  • Help ensure funds are allocated to projects that build community and school spirit.
  • Be approachable and open to feedback—I want to hear what matters to you and make sure your ideas are supported.

I love the CSSS and the energy that our community brings to SFU. If elected, I’ll do my best to help keep things running smoothly behind the scenes so you can focus on learning, making friends, and having fun.

Thanks for reading,
Matthew

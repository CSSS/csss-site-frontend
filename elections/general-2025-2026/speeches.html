CSSS 2025-2026 General Election

What are all these positions in the first place? You can read more about it in the CSSS's Constitution.

Voting for this election will be conducted through SFU's simplyvoting platform. If you are elligible, you will be sent an email with instructions on how to vote (login to sfu.simplyvoting.com and select this election).

You are elligible to vote if you are a declared majors, minors, or honours in any program of study offered at the undergraduate level by the School of Computing Science, or are currently enrolled in any undergraduate course offered by the School of Computing Science.

(1) President

Candidate Name: Mabel Ling
Candidate Speech

Hi everyone!

Over the last 3 years, the CSSS has become my second home, and I've made many friends and met many amazing, passionate people. During my time here, I served a number of positions. First as a volunteer, then as Exec-at-Large, then as Council Rep, and now as Treasurer. I hope my experience in each of these roles have created a strong foundation in understanding how our student society functions, and how we connect with the faculty, the SFSS, clubs, professors, and the student body.

In my past roles, I have been responsible for helping in various logistics in multiple iterations of our large events: Frosh Week, Tech Fair, Fall Hacks, Mountain Madness, FAS Formal, the Silicon Valley Trip, and many other events as well.

This year, I have been predominantly responsible for financial oversight in nearly every aspect of the CSSS. From our large-scale events to each and every board games night, I have been balancing the budget and working on funding to help bring as many events as possible to you. In addition to that, I have been involved on a number of other projects over the last few months, including the common room move, the workshops on microcontrollers, and our summer touch grass hike and dessert crawl.

During my presidency, I intend to oversee the following:
  • Developing relations with professors, staff, and the faculty as a whole
  • Strengthening the community through our events through collaborations with other clubs and student societies
  • Expanding external company and alumni contact networks towards improving educational and career-oriented opportunities
  • Upgrading the common room space for bettering campus life and connecting with students individually
A lot of time and effort is required to run the society, but this community is important to me, and I wish to have a hand in continuing to improve the SFU experience for CS students. As your president, I hope be your voice for the upcoming year.

Thank you,
Mabel Ling

Candidate Name: Elvin Zukic
Candidate Speech
Hi everyone!

My name is Elvin Zukic, and I am excited to be running to be your CSSS Treasurer for the 2025-26 term. As the current Director of Educational Events I have had the honor of serving you all and the CSSS through organizing and hosting events such as Tech Workshops for Dummies and the FAS Formal.
I want to keep that momentum going as I transition in the Treasurer role. As the Treasurer, there are a few key challenges I want to tackle:

More Funding Options
As the SFSS becomes less reliable for funding assistance, we must look to other funding options to support Computer Science events. A major new source of funding would be obtaining sponsorships from large tech companies in the lower mainland. Not only would this allow us to run larger, more fleshed-out events but it will also allow us to build valuable relationships with these companies. 
Offering more merch options will also be a strong revenue stream as well as allowing our community more opportunities to proudly represent Computer Science.

Empowering Student Clubs 
      The CSSS is not the sole organization in our community, we have a multitude of other clubs in the Computing Science community such as Developers and Systems Club (DNS), Google Developer Student Club (GDSC), SFU Surge, MechaKeys, GameDev Club, Competitive Programming and so many more. These clubs do amazing work with their events, and I want to support them financially so they can make these events bigger and better. By empowering student clubs, we can build a stronger community in Computing Science.  

Faster Reimbursements 
As someone who runs events for my other clubs at SFU (Toastmasters and SFUMTG), I find myself footing most of the costs of the event and being frustrated when it takes a while for the SFSS to reimburse me. I do not want this happening to our event organizers as well.
As the Treasurer I will prioritize getting money back to the event organizers who foot the bill for our events. It’s the least I could do given all the hours and effort they put in to making their events amazing. 

I not only want to make the CSSS stronger financially but also the entirety of student-run Computing Science. I hope you can put your faith in me and believe that together, we can build a community that will be stronger than ever.

Thank you for your consideration.

Best Regards
